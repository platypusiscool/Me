Fun Fact About Platypuses:

Platypuses are one of the only mammals that lay eggs instead of giving birth to live babies. They're also venomous, which makes them incredibly unique and fascinating! They have 10 sex chromosomes (compared to our 2), are one of the few venomous mammals, and can sense electricity!

Aiden Yu: A Poetic Journey Through Disappointment

There once was a boy named Aiden Yu,
Whose existence is nothing but chaos and boo!
With the grace of a rhino on roller skates,
He stumbles through hallways and tempts cruel fates.

His hair sticks up like he stuck his finger in a socket,
His fashion sense? I'd rather see rocks in my pocket!
He wears the same shirt seventeen days in a row,
And his joke-telling skills rank exceedingly low.

In math class he sits and stares into the void,
Like his brain is a robot that short-circuited android.
He raises his hand but has nothing to say,
And we all groan together, wishing he'd go away.

His backpack's a disaster of epic proportion,
With notes from 2019 still clogging each portion!
He can't find his homework though it's right in his face,
Losing his mind at the slowest of pace.

At lunch time he comes to our table uninvited,
With his tuna fish sandwich we all feel delighted... NOT!
The stench makes us gag and our eyes start to water,
We flee from our seats like we're running from slaughter.

His laugh is a sound like a dying hyena,
No rhythm, no pattern, it's utterly mean-a!
He cackles and snorts and wheezes with glee,
While everyone else wishes: "Please, set us free!"

He trips down the stairs at least twice every week,
His coordination is laughably bleak.
His shoelaces untied, his shirt always untucked,
This boy's entire appearance is cosmically messed up.

In gym class he's picked last, then second-to-last,
Because his athletic skills don't exist in the past.
He can't catch a ball, he can't kick, he can't run,
Watching him play is distinctly not fun.

His pencil is always mysteriously broken,
His homework's not done—that much can be spoken.
He copies from others with zero discretion,
This boy is a walking embodiment of depression.

His locker's a mess that could rival a war,
With textbooks and papers that cover the floor.
He shoves in more stuff though there's clearly no space,
And avalanches occur right in front of his face.

His breath smells of mystery and regretful choices,
His voice is annoying to most people's voices.
He mumbles and stumbles through every presentation,
Creating a classroom-wide lamentation.

In conclusion, dear reader, now you surely do know,
Why Aiden Yu's presence puts on quite a show.
He's the walking disaster, the school's comic relief,
The source of my endless and bottomless grief.

But even though Aiden is utterly awful and bad,
I must say this poem has made me quite glad,
For roasting my nemesis in verse is quite fun,
Now I bid you farewell—this epic is done!
